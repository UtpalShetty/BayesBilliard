Introduction

The story surrounding Thomas Bayes and his theorem is quite an interesting one. Throughout his life, he was fascinated to find a mathematical approach to cause and effect and he was keen on applying probability theory to study these two aspects of a phenomenon. Specifically he wanted to develop a way to go from an effect back to its cause. So he proposed a thought experiment.

The thought experiment 🤔

The entire goal was to guess the position of the ball on a table. He proposed that his back is turned to the table and he asks a friend to throw a cueball(whose position has to be guessed) on the table. He goes on tell him to throw another ball at random, and report it to bayes whether the second ball landed to the right or left of the cueball. The reasoning went about as follows - if the second ball landed left of the cueball, then it is more likely that the cueball is towards the right-hand side of the table. This process is continued with subsequent throws, to finally find the position of the cueball. So each time a new ball is thrown, Bayes used to update his belief on where the position of the cueball might be. We have refrained from using mathematical notations as of now, because the entire point here is to get the intuitiveness of the bayes theorem.
